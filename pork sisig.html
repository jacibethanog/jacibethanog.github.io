Pork sisig

Ingredients:

1 lb pork belly or pork jowl, boiled and chopped into small pieces

1 onion, chopped

3 cloves garlic, minced

3 to 5 pieces red chili peppers (siling labuyo), chopped

1/4 cup calamansi juice (or lemon juice)

3 tbsp soy sauce

2 tbsp mayonnaise

1 egg

Salt and pepper to taste

2 tbsp cooking oil

2 tbsp butter (optional)

1 green chili pepper (optional), chopped

Chopped green onions and sliced calamansi for garnish


Instructions:

1.In a large bowl, combine the chopped pork belly, calamansi juice, soy sauce, minced garlic, and chopped red chili peppers. Mix well and marinate for at least 30 minutes.

2.In a skillet or frying pan, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Add the marinated pork mixture (including the marinade) and cook until the pork is crispy and slightly browned, stirring occasionally. This usually takes about 10 to 15 minutes.

3.Once the pork is crispy, add the chopped onion to the skillet and continue to cook for another 2 to 3 minutes until the onion is soft and translucent.

4.Push the pork mixture to one side of the skillet and crack the egg onto the other side. Scramble the egg until it's fully cooked, then mix it with the pork mixture.

5.Add the butter to the skillet (if using) and mix well until melted. This step is optional but adds extra flavor and richness to the sisig.

6.Season with salt and pepper to taste. You can also add chopped green chili peppers for an extra kick of spice.

7.Turn off the heat and transfer the pork sisig to a serving plate. Drizzle with mayonnaise and garnish with chopped green onions and sliced calamansi.

8.Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy your delicious Pork Sisig!
