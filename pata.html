Crispy Pata

Ingredients:

1 whole pork leg (pata), about 3-4 lbs

3-4 cups water

2-3 pieces bay leaves

4 cloves garlic, crushed

1 medium onion, quartered

1 tbsp whole peppercorns

1 tbsp salt

2-3 cups cooking oil (for deep-frying)

For serving: soy sauce, vinegar, and chili dipping sauce


Instructions:

1.Clean the pork leg thoroughly under running water and pat it dry with paper towels.

2.In a large pot, combine the water, bay leaves, crushed garlic, quartered onion, whole peppercorns, and salt. Add the pork leg to the pot and bring the water to a boil.

3.Once boiling, lower the heat to a simmer and cover the pot. Let the pork leg cook for about 1.5 to 2 hours or until the meat is tender. You can also use a pressure cooker to speed up the cooking process.

4.Once the pork leg is tender, carefully remove it from the pot and let it cool down slightly. You can also refrigerate it for a few hours or overnight to dry out the skin, which will result in crispier skin when fried.

5.Heat the cooking oil in a deep fryer or a large pot to 350°F (175°C).

6.Carefully lower the pork leg into the hot oil, skin side down. Fry it for about 10-15 minutes or until the skin is golden brown and crispy. Make sure to turn the pork leg occasionally for even cooking.

7.Once crispy and golden brown, remove the Crispy Pata from the oil and drain it on paper towels to remove excess oil.

8.Transfer the Crispy Pata to a serving plate and serve it hot with soy sauce, vinegar, and chili dipping sauce on the side.

9.Enjoy your delicious and crispy Filipino Crispy Pata!
