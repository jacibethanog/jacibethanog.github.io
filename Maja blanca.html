Maja Blanca

Ingredients:

1 cup cornstarch

1 can (400ml) coconut milk

1 can (370ml) evaporated milk

1 can (300ml) condensed milk

1 cup granulated sugar

1 cup sweet corn kernels (optional, for texture and flavor)

1 teaspoon vanilla extract

1/2 cup toasted grated coconut (for topping)

1 tablespoon butter (for greasing)


Instructions:

1.Grease a baking dish or mold with butter and set aside

2.In a bowl, dissolve the cornstarch in 1 cup of water until smooth and free of lumps. Set aside.

3.In a large saucepan or pot, combine the coconut milk, evaporated milk, condensed milk, and granulated sugar. Stir well until the sugar is dissolved.

4.Place the saucepan over medium heat and bring the mixture to a gentle boil, stirring constantly to prevent scorching.

5.Once the mixture is boiling, gradually pour in the dissolved cornstarch mixture while stirring continuously. Keep stirring until the mixture thickens and becomes smooth and glossy.

6.Add the sweet corn kernels (if using) and continue to cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly.

7.Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract until well combined.

8.Pour the mixture into the greased baking dish or mold, spreading it evenly.

9.Allow the Maja Blanca to cool and set at room temperature for about 30 minutes to 1 hour.

10.Once set, sprinkle the toasted grated coconut evenly over the top of the Maja Blanca.

11.You can also refrigerate the Maja Blanca for a few hours or overnight to chill and set further before serving. Garnish with additional toasted grated coconut or grated cheese if desired.

12.Slice into squares or rectangles, then serve and enjoy your delicious Filipino Maja Blanca dessert!
