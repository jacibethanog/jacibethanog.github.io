Pinakbet

Ingredients:

1 medium-sized bitter melon (ampalaya), sliced

1 medium-sized eggplant, sliced

1 medium-sized squash (kalabasa), cubed

10-12 pieces okra, ends trimmed

1 cup string beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch lengths

1 medium-sized tomato, diced

1 medium-sized onion, chopped

3-4 cloves garlic, minced

1/4 cup shrimp paste (bagoong)

2 tablespoons cooking oil

1 cup water

Salt and pepper to taste


Instructions:

1.Heat cooking oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Saute garlic until golden brown, then add onions and cook until translucent.

2.Add tomatoes to the pan and cook until softened.

3.Stir in the shrimp paste (bagoong) and cook for 1-2 minutes to release its flavors.

4.Add the squash (kalabasa) to the pan and cook for 3-4 minutes until slightly tender.

5.Add the bitter melon (ampalaya) and cook for another 2-3 minutes.

6.Pour in the water and bring to a simmer. Cover the pan and cook for 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are halfway cooked.

7.Add the eggplant, okra, and string beans to the pan. Stir gently to combine with the other vegetables.

8.Cover the pan again and continue simmering until all the vegetables are tender, about 5-7 minutes.

9.Season with salt and pepper to taste, adjusting the flavors as needed.

10.Once the vegetables are cooked and the flavors have melded together, remove from heat and transfer to a serving dish.

11.Serve the Pinakbet hot with steamed rice. Enjoy the delicious and nutritious Filipino vegetable dish!
